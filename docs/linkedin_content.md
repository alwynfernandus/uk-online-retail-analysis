**Project Title:** UK Online Retail — End-to-End Business Analysis

**Description:**

Analysed 13 months of transaction data (541,909 rows) from a UK-based e-commerce retailer to
identify revenue drivers, customer churn risk, and growth opportunities.

Built a full analytical pipeline covering data cleaning, KPI definition, RFM customer segmentation,
cohort retention analysis, product performance benchmarking, and geographic revenue breakdown —
resulting in six actionable business insights and a prioritised set of strategic recommendations.

**Key highlights:**
- Identified £931K in at-risk revenue from a recoverable customer segment
- Discovered that 55% of revenue is concentrated in a single customer tier — a significant
  retention dependency
- Found that 35% of customers are one-time buyers, pointing to a post-purchase experience gap
- Mapped peak order windows (Tue–Thu, 10am–2pm) to optimise marketing spend allocation

**Tools:** Python · pandas · matplotlib · seaborn · scikit-learn

**Dataset:** UCI Online Retail Dataset (publicly available)

I just published an end-to-end Business Analysis project on GitHub — and I want to share what I
actually found.

The dataset: 13 months of transactions from a UK online gift retailer. 541,909 rows. 37 countries.
£8.7M in revenue.

Here's what the data showed:

**55% of revenue comes from a single customer tier.**
Champions — customers who buy often, recently, and in large amounts — generate more than half the
business. If 10% of them leave, that's a six-figure hole. The company had no retention program for them.

**£931K is sitting in a recoverable "At Risk" segment.**
These were once high-spending customers who've gone quiet. A basic re-engagement campaign with a
15% success rate recovers ~£140K. That's not a data science problem — it's a CRM execution problem.

**35% of customers never came back.**
One purchase, then gone. The data doesn't tell you why, but it tells you the problem is real.
Post-purchase experience needs work.

**Marketing spend is wasted on weekends.**
Order volumes on Saturdays and Sundays are a fraction of midweek levels. The buyer profile here is
B2B — wholesalers ordering during business hours. Weekend ad spend is dead budget.
